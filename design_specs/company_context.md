Lumicello — Company Overview (For Web Design Team)

Reference: internal brand & messaging document  ￼

⸻

1. What Lumicello Is

Lumicello is a personalized learning platform for children and families.
It blends research-backed guidance, AI-driven customization, and adaptive pacing to create individualized learning journeys — digital and physical.

Parents and educators use Lumicello to understand what truly interests a child, track progress, and support them with the right resources at the right time.

Children experience Lumicello through:
	•	A friendly, curiosity-based learning app
	•	Personalized learning “quests”
	•	Exploration paths based on the child’s evolving interests
	•	Hands-on physical toy kits ("LumiBox") — similar to Lovevery but tuned to each child's curiosity development

Lumicello is built for learners, parents, and educators, not just tech-savvy users.

⸻

2. Core Philosophy

Lumicello is built around three beliefs:

1) Research-Backed

Technology amplifies teacher wisdom, not replaces it.
Parents and educators get insight into each child's unique learning pattern so they can guide them better.

2) Customized

Each child has a Curiosity Fingerprint™ — a multidimensional profile that evolves over time and informs:
	•	Recommended activities
	•	Learning styles
	•	Interest clusters
	•	Depth of engagement

3) Right Pace

Children develop at different speeds.
Lumicello adapts difficulty, exploration depth, and learning tempo dynamically.

Outcome:
A child who stays challenged, confident, and excited — never overwhelmed, never bored.

⸻

3. Product Surface Areas

A. Digital Platform

The heart of Lumicello.

Key features:
	•	Personalized discovery dashboard
	•	Adaptive learning recommendations
	•	Interest graph & progress visualization (Constellation and Fingerprint motif)
	•	Interactive micro-lessons
	•	Achievement moments (Lumen Gold highlight)

B. Parent Experience

Parents get:
	•	Weekly “What we noticed” insights
	•	Strengths/interest mapping
	•	Suggestions for at-home activities
	•	Guidance on pacing and environment
	•	Optional email newsletter for curated learning ideas

Tone: warm, encouraging, advisory — not technical.

C. Physical Toy Boxes ("LumiBox")

A new product line, inspired by Lovevery but personalized:

Examples:
	•	Kits matched to child’s interest clusters (e.g., nature, building, movement, music)
	•	Activities printed with Lumicello’s visual identity
	•	Cards introducing “quests” linked back to the app
	•	Age-appropriate materials supporting developmental milestones

The website must show:
	•	Visuals of curated kits
	•	Child-friendly photography
	•	Clear “for parents” explanations of developmental benefits

5. Visual Identity (Simplified for Designers)

Primary Colors
	•	Jefferson Blue — #232D4B (anchor, trustworthy)
	•	Rotunda White — #F7F6F2 (clean background)
	•	Lumen Gold — #FFD447 (highlights, CTAs)

Secondary Colors
	•	Brick (#C65D32)
	•	Lawn Green (#5C8D4E)
	•	Sky Blue (#4F91CD)
	•	Slate Gray (#5E6A71)

Typography
	•	Headlines: Noto Serif / Crimson Pro
	•	Body: Noto Sans / Inter (Thai support via Noto Sans Thai)
	•	Tech elements: JetBrains Mono (sparingly)

Shapes & Patterns
	•	Constellations: dots connected by soft lines
	•	Fingerprint Flows: flowing lines representing individual paths
	•	Adaptive Grid: clean cards, rounded corners

Illustration Style
	•	Warm, geometric, minimalist; 3–4 color palette per scene
	•	Child-friendly but not childish
	•	Plenty of white space

Photography Style
	•	Real families, natural lighting
	•	Authentic moments of curiosity
	•	Avoid stock-photo artificiality

⸻

6. Motion / Interaction
	•	Soft glows (Lumen Gold) for achievement
	•	Subtle flow animations between sections
	•	Gentle reveals (fade + slide)
	•	“Connection drawing” animation for constellation patterns

Nothing flashy or overstimulating — especially important for child/parent UX.

7. Suggested Website Structure

Hero Section
	•	Big friendly headline ("Unlocking the Genius in Every Child")
	•	Subtext: short, warm, parent-centered
	•	Visual: Abstract 3D shapes representing curiosity
	•	Primary CTA: "Join our Community"

Community Section (immediately after Hero)
	•	Social links (Line, Instagram) as prominent cards
	•	Newsletter signup form with Name + Email
	•	Prioritizes community building and early engagement

Value Props
	•	3 cards: "Research-Backed," "Customized," "Right Pace"
	•	Icons: Soft, colorful SVG icons

Curiosity Fingerprint™
	•	Explain in parent-friendly terms
	•	Visual: constellation/radar chart with floating animation

LumiBox (Toy Boxes)
	•	3-column grid of physical kits
	•	"Coming Soon" badges for pre-launch phase
	•	Kit examples: Nature Explorer, Structural Engineer, Music Maker

Testimonials / Impact
	•	Parent quotes
	•	Large text, centered, clean design

Footer
	•	Simple columns with navigation links
	•	Brand tagline: "Every child has a light inside. We help it shine."
