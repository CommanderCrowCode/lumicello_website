3 Quick Ways to Spark Curiosity

While you wait for your first email, try these simple activities with your little one.

1

The "What If" Game

Next time you're reading a book together, pause and ask "What do you think happens next?" There's no wrong answer. This simple question builds prediction skills and creative thinking.

2

Kitchen Scientists

Let your child help with cooking (age-appropriate tasks!). Measuring, pouring, and mixing are all early math and science concepts in disguise. Plus, kids eat better when they help prepare food.

3

Wonder Walks

On your next walk, let your child lead. Stop when they stop. Look at what catches their attention. Ask questions together. Some of the best learning happens when we follow their curiosity, not our agenda.
